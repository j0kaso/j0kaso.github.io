Basics of classical MDs

Fundamental equation of classical molecular dynamics: Newton's laws of motion

The forces is given by the negative gradient of the potential energy.
Fi = - ∂Utotal / ∂ri

Knowing the force allows us to accelerate the atoms in the direction of the force
Fi = m ai

Potential energy calculated from bonded & non-bonded interactions
Utotal = Ubonded + Unon-bonded

Force Fields

⇒ Energy of atoms by Utotal & parameters

costs ⇓ complexity

Simplified description:

  • Fixed point charges ⇒ no electronic polarization
  • Classical mechanics ⇒ no isotope effects
  • Fixed bond topology ⇒ no chemistry

⇓

Specific force fields for specific questions!

experimentally-derived: vdW radii, torsions, bond length, angles, charges, atomic masses

Force fields are parameterized to compensate for their simplified description of reality

  • fixed point charges (no electronic polarization) ⇒ increase partial charges to recover polarization effects
  • Classical mechanics (no isotpe effects) ⇒ tune vdW parameters to revocer the experimental density
  • Fixed bond topology (no chemistry)
  • Environment
  • Molecule type & behavior
  • Time-scales

CAN & SHOULD be optimized / parameterized (by quantum mechanics)

Basics of classical MDs

Fi = m ai = - ∂Utotal / ∂ri

  • Assign masses & initial velocities
  • Simulation loop
    • update neighbour list
    • calculate forces F(r)
    • solve equation of motion
      • update position and velocity
      • include constraints
    • perform p, T control
    • data output
  • Resulting trajectory analysis

⇒ Calcuating forces is expensive

  1. Integrate Newton's equations of motion
  2. Stop & evaluate energy
  3. repeat 2 & 3 = timestep/iteration
  • ⇒ atomic forces & masses used to solve positions of each atom at a time
  • trajectory = resulting series of snapshots of structural changes over time
  • initial velocities = total kinetic energy of the system dictated by a desired simulation temperature (from Maxwell-Boltzmann / Gaussian distribution)
  • force on atom calculated form change in energy between current position & its position a small distance away
  • "time step" = 1 fsec = fastest bond vibration
  • SHAKE-algorithm fixes H-bonds allowing the use of a 2fsec time step

Integrators/Algorithms

⇒ How to make a step!

  • Gradient Ascent (e.g. Steepest Descent):
    → zig-zag
  • Langevin dynamics (stochastic dynamics):
    → friction force & random force
  • Metropolis Monte Carlo method (Metropolis-Hastings):
    → following a probability by Metropolis criterion
  • ...

  • Gradient Ascent: e.g. Steepest Descent; minimum approached in a zig-zag manner with search direction orthogonal to previous step
  • Langevin dynamics (stochastic dynamics): Particles experience a friction force as well as a random force from particles "outside" the simulation
  • Metropolis Monte Carlo method (Metropolis-Hastings): Generate any trial move you like, and accept/reject the move with a probability Given by the Metropolis criterion
